# Interview policy

Ask one question at a time. Target five to seven questions and stop at ten. Show simple progress only when it helps: “Question 3 of roughly 6.” Honor “you decide” by making and explaining a recommendation from current evidence.

Prioritize: concrete lead or repeated friction; affected user and moment; current workaround; domain access; desired outcome; prior builds and preferences; constraints; and smallest demand test. Skip a question when authorized evidence already answers its factual portion. Ask preferences directly even when GitHub evidence exists.

Use plain language. A first-time builder receives the same rigor, with terms explained at the moment they matter. Never turn the interview into a resume quiz.
